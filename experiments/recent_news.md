## RECENT NEWS ARTICLES

**1. "Fifth Circuit Grants DOL Request to Dismiss Fiduciary Rule Appeals"**
*Source: 401k Specialist | Date: December 1, 2025*
The Department of Labor (DOL) under the new administration has formally withdrawn its defense of the Biden-era "Retirement Security Rule." This dismissal effectively ends the litigation that sought to expand the definition of a fiduciary to include one-time rollover advice, signaling a return to the 1975 five-part test for fiduciary status.

**2. "New SEC Regulation S‑P Amendments: Compliance Deadlines for Private Fund Managers"**
*Source: EisnerAmper | Date: December 1, 2025*
Amendments to Regulation S-P regarding consumer financial privacy and incident response are set to become effective for larger entities this week. The updated rule mandates that registered investment advisers and broker-dealers implement written incident response programs and notify customers of sensitive data breaches within 30 days.

**3. "FinCEN Formally Proposes Two-Year Delay of Investment Adviser AML Rule"**
*Source: Regulatory Compliance | Date: November 2025*
The Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) and the SEC have indicated a postponement of the compliance date for the new Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Customer Identification Program (CIP) requirements for investment advisers. The new timeline pushes full implementation to January 1, 2028, to allow for broader regulatory reconsideration.

**4. "SEC Spring 2025 Agenda: A Focus on Deregulation and Crypto Assets"**
*Source: Katten | Date: September 2025*
The SEC’s latest regulatory agenda, released under Chairman Paul Atkins, prioritizes "capital formation" over new disclosure mandates. Key items include potential updates to the "accredited investor" definition to expand access to private markets and a renewed focus on establishing clear custody and trading rules for digital assets.

**5. "SFDR 2.0: European Commission Proposes Shift to Product Categorization"**
*Source: Sidley Austin | Date: November 25, 2025*
The European Commission has published a proposal to overhaul the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR), moving from a disclosure-based regime to a formal product categorization system. The proposal introduces specific labels for "Transition" and "Sustainable" products, requiring funds to meet a 70% investment threshold to use sustainability-related marketing terms.

**6. "EU Postpones Market Risk Capital Requirements (FRTB) to 2027"**
*Source: European Commission | Date: June/November 2025*
Citing delays in US implementation, the European Commission has adopted a delegated act to postpone the "Fundamental Review of the Trading Book" (FRTB) market risk rules under Basel III until January 1, 2027. The move aims to preserve a level playing field for EU banks' trading desks against their global peers.

**7. "European Parliament Adopts Resolution on AI in the Financial Sector"**
*Source: Regulation Tomorrow | Date: November 27, 2025*
The European Parliament has passed a resolution calling for specific guidance on the application of AI in financial services, emphasizing the need to mitigate "herding" behavior and concentration risks. The resolution urges supervisory authorities to apply a "sliding scale" of scrutiny based on the sensitivity of the AI use case (e.g., credit scoring vs. marketing).

**8. "OECD Forecast: Global Growth to Slow as Tariffs Take Effect"**
*Source: Morningstar/Dow Jones | Date: December 2, 2025*
The OECD’s latest quarterly report projects a slowdown in US and global GDP growth for 2026 as recently implemented tariffs on critical inputs begin to impact supply chains. However, the report notes that accelerated capital expenditure in Artificial Intelligence could partially offset the drag from trade friction.

**9. "SCOTUS Reviews Trump Tariffs Under IEEPA"**
*Source: JD Supra | Date: December 2, 2025*
The Supreme Court is currently hearing arguments in *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump*, challenging the executive branch's authority to impose tariffs under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA). A ruling against the administration could significantly alter the President’s leverage in ongoing trade negotiations and impact the cost basis for consumer goods importers.

**10. "FSOC Improvement Act 2025 Gains Industry Support"**
*Source: SIFMA | Date: October 30, 2025*
Trade associations are rallying behind bipartisan legislation that would reform how the Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC) designates non-bank financial institutions as "systemically important." The bill seeks to make the designation process more transparent and requires FSOC to prioritize an "activities-based" approach over targeting individual asset managers.

**11. "Federal Reserve Supervision Report: Unrealized Losses Persist"**
*Source: Federal Reserve | Date: December 1, 2025*
The Fed’s biannual Supervision and Regulation Report notes that while the banking sector remains well-capitalized, hold-to-maturity securities portfolios still contain approximately $250 billion in unrealized losses. The report highlights continued supervisory focus on liquidity readiness and interest rate risk management at regional banks.

**12. "Tokenized Collateral and Custody Focus for 2026"**
*Source: SIFMA/SEC | Date: November 2025*
Industry groups have submitted requests for exemptive relief to facilitate the use of tokenized securities as collateral in derivatives markets. Concurrently, the SEC is soliciting feedback on modernizing the "Custody Rule" to explicitly address how registered advisers should safeguard client digital assets, a key friction point for institutional crypto adoption.
